Reflection:​

I think my eating is in the middle — not super healthy, but also not really bad. I eat fruits, vegetables, and home-cooked food most of the time. I really like avocado toast, salads, and sometimes sandwiches with ham and provolone. I also eat school lunch, which is okay. I do eat fast food maybe once every two weeks when we go out, like on Saturdays. I also like small sweet things like Rice Krispies. I drink mostly water or juice.

Experts say teens my age usually need about 2,000 to 2,400 calories a day depending on how active we are. I think I get a little less because I'm not super active, so I think it's okay for me.

I could improve by eating fewer processed foods like Rice Krispies and chicken nuggets, and more whole foods like rice, beans, and cooked veggies. I can also add more protein like eggs or lean meat.

SMART Goal for Nutrition:​

S – I want to eat more home-cooked meals and cut back on fast food and processed snacks.
M – I will eat fast food no more than once every two weeks and only have one sugary snack (like Rice Krispies) per school day.
A – I can ask my parents to help plan healthy dinners and take healthy snacks to school.
R – Yes, I already eat some healthy meals and this is not a big change.
T – I will follow this goal for 2 months and then check how I feel.
